New Law Gives Government Power to Temporarily Restrict Visa Arrivals

March 2026

Australia enacted the Migration Amendment (2026 Measures No. 1) Act on 14 March 2026, empowering the Immigration Minister to issue "arrival control determinations" — temporarily pausing arrivals of specific offshore temporary visa groups for up to six months. The Act was first applied in late March 2026 to restrict Iranian Visitor (Subclass 600) visa holders offshore from travelling to Australia.

Points Test Overhaul: What Skilled Migrants Need to Know Before July 2026

June 2026

As part of the 2026–27 Federal Budget, the Australian Government announced a reform of the Skilled Migration Points Test expected to take effect in July 2026. The changes will favour younger applicants with superior English proficiency and may introduce income-based points. Subclass 189, 190, and 491 visa applicants are most likely to be affected.

Student Visas

Australia Introduces 'Genuine Student' Test and Higher English Bar for Student Visas

February 2026

A new Genuine Student (GS) requirement now replaces the Genuine Temporary Entrant test, asking applicants to demonstrate a clear link between their course choice, academic background, and career goals. Minimum IELTS scores have risen to 6.0 overall, and India, Nepal, Bangladesh, and Bhutan have been reclassified to Evidence Level 3 — the strictest tier in Australia's Simplified Student Visa Framework.
